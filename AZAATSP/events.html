Upcoming Events

AATSP Arizona invites you to attend two workshops during the month of February. Registration is open for both workshops and additional information about each follows.

AZLA Spring 2016 Workshop

Saturday, February 20, 2016 from 9:00 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. at Cactus Shadows High School in Cave Creek AZ MAP

All K-16 World Language educators, students, administrators, and friends of the profession are cordially invited to attend the ARIZONA LANGUAGE ASSOCIATION’s one-day Spring Workshop, graciously hosted by Cactus Shadows High School. This year’s workshop centers on familiarizing our attendees with and working to implement The NEW Arizona World and Native Languages Standards

Come and explore the updated K-12 Arizona World and Native Languages Standards with a group of educators who facilitated these revisions. This workshop will introduce the newly-revised state standards, noting significant changes and offering strategies that align with these standards. Participants will have a chance to get up close and personal with the new Arizona World and Native Language Standards, collaborate with colleagues and work together to develop materials for immediate implementation.

Gail’s Educational Materials (GEM) Sale: Bring teaching materials of any kind to donate to colleagues’ classrooms, and/or make a small donation to select something “new” for your students’ educational experience. Donations benefit the Gail Guntermann Professional Development Fellowship. Drawings will reward lucky AZLA participants with gift certificates! Certificates for Professional Development hours will be issued if requested at registration.

Make sure to register online by February 5, 2016 to avoid a late fee and reserve your breakfast and lunch!

Fernando Rubio Workshop

Saturday, February 27, 2016 at Rio Salado Conference Center, 2323 W 14th Street, Tempe AZ 85281


El desarrollo de la comunicación interpersonal oral

En este taller discutiremos el proceso y la transición desde la actuación (performance) a la competencia (proficiency) en el desarollo de las destrezas orales a todos los niveles. El taller incluye práctica con estrategias de enseñanza y aprendizaje que facilitan el desarrollo de la comunicación interpersonal oral.

Fernando Rubio is Associate Professor of Spanish Linguistics at the University of Utah, where he is also Co-Director of the Second Language Teaching and Research Center and Director of Online Curriculum Enhancement and Innovation. His research interests are in the areas of Applied Linguistics and Teaching Methodologies including assessment and technology-enhanced language learning and teaching. He is an ACTFL OPI tester and trainer, AAPPL rater and trainer and serves as co-chair of the AP Spanish Language and Culture Development Committee.

Breakfast and Registration from 8:30 - 8:50 a.m. Workshop 9:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m.

Cost: AATSP Members $30.00, Non AATSP Members $40.00, Students $20.00
